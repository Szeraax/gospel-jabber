How to Play

Gospel Jabber is a word or phrase guessing party game, similar to Hasbro's Catch Phrase, with a Latter-Day Saint theme. You will need at least two players (and ideally a large group), a phone or other mobile device to pass around the group, and a means of keeping score, if desired.

During play, one person, holding the device, tries to give clues to their team to get them to guess the secret, randomly-chosen phrase. The person giving clues may not use the secret phrase (or any words within the phrase) while giving clues. If their team correctly guesses the phrase before the timer expires, then they pass the device to another person; this repeats until the timer expires.

Traditionally, the players divide into two even teams. One player starts the game and describes their phrase while their teammates guess the phrase; after their team guesses the phrase correctly, then the player passes the device to a member of the other team, who then gives clues to their team. This repeats, alternating between teams, until the timer expires, and the team that was not guessing when the time ran out wins a point.

Using this app

There are two game modes available: "traditional," which is the standard timed "hot potato" mode; and "casual," which is an untimed mode suitable for a non-competitive activity.

There are several potential categories that can be selected on the start page. The "Everything" category has all of the phrases from every category. The "Children's list" category contains a subset of terms from the various categories, picked to be more suitable for younger or less-experienced players.

After selecting the desired mode and category, the "Start" button will take you to the main game page. The first person to give clues presses the green "next" button to start a round and gives their clues; after their team guesses the phrase, they pass the device to the next person who presses the "next" button again to select a new phrase. When the time runs out, a buzzer will sound.

Scorekeeping is not built into the app, so if you want to keep score you will need to do that separately.
